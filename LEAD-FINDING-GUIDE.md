# Lead Finding Guide — Free Methods

## METHOD 1: LinkedIn (Best for Quality)

### Search queries:
- "n8n automation" + UK
- "workflow automation" + SMB + UK
- "AI automation" + marketing agency
- "digital transformation" + manufacturing

### What to look for:
- Posts mentioning automation problems
- Companies hiring automation roles
- Decision makers: CTO, Head of Ops, COO

### Daily routine (20 mins):
1. Search one query
2. Find 5 relevant prospects
3. View profile, find pain point
4. Send personalized connection + message
5. Track in spreadsheet

## METHOD 2: Apollo.io (Free Tier)

- 50 free contact credits/month
- Search by: industry + job title + company size
- Target: Marketing agencies, recruitment firms, B2B SaaS
- Export leads to Google Sheets

## METHOD 3: n8n Community Forum

Go to community.n8n.io
- Find posts where companies are stuck
- Help them solve problems
- They'll ask about hiring you
- No selling needed — just be helpful

## METHOD 4: n8n Partner Program

- Visit n8n.io/partners
- Find partner agencies
- Message them about subcontracting
- They have clients, you do the work
- 50/50 split or fixed fee

## METHOD 5: Reddit

Subreddits to watch:
- r/n8n
- r/nocode
- r/automation
- r/entrepreneur

When someone posts about automation problems:
1. Give genuine helpful answer
2. Mention your background briefly
3. Offer to help further via DM

## Target Client Profile

IDEAL CLIENTS:
- Company size: 10-200 employees
- Industries: Marketing agencies, recruitment, B2B SaaS
- Pain: Manual repetitive processes
- Budget: $1,000-$5,000 for automation
- Location: UK, US, India, Europe (remote)

NOT IDEAL:
- 1-2 person startups (no budget)
- Large enterprises (long sales cycle)
- Pure retail/physical businesses
